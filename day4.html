The forth day of Navratri: Chaturthi

Kushmanda is the fourth form of Goddess Durga. Her name is derived from three words: "Ku" means "little," "Ushma" means "warmth," and "Anda" means "cosmic egg." Kushmanda is believed to have created the universe with her divine smile, and she is often depicted as having eight or ten hands, holding various objects such as a lotus, a bow, and an arrow. She is the source of all energy and life.

Dress Code of the Day: Red

Chaturthi is a day of devotion to Goddess Kushmanda. It is an opportunity for devotees to seek her blessings for vitality, strength, and prosperity. The red color of the day symbolizes the dynamic and life-giving nature of the Goddess.

Artist for Pratipada: Geeta Rabari

Geeta Rabari is a popular Indian folk singer, primarily known for her work in the Gujarati music industry. She gained recognition and popularity for her folk and traditional music, particularly in the Garba and Raas music genres, which are celebrated during festivals like Navratri in Gujarat. Geeta Rabari's music is characterized by its folk influences, traditional melodies, and lyrics often inspired by rural life and culture. She is known for her melodious voice and has a significant following among audiences in Gujarat and among the Gujarati diaspora.
