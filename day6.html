The sixth day of Navratri: Shashtmi

Katyayani is the sixth form of Goddess Durga. She is known for her fierce and warrior-like appearance. According to Hindu mythology, she was born to the sage Katyayan and is a symbol of courage and bravery. She is often depicted with four arms, holding a sword and a lotus, and riding a lion. Devotees seek her blessings for strength and courage.

Dress Code of the Day: Yellow

Shashti is a day of devotion to Goddess Katyayani. It is an opportunity for devotees to seek her blessings for courage, strength, and the ability to face life's challenges with determination. The yellow color of the day symbolizes the happiness and enthusiasm associated with this form of the Goddess.The color associated with Shashti is yellow. Yellow signifies happiness, brightness, and joy. It reflects the vibrant and cheerful nature of Goddess Katyayani and is worn to invoke a sense of optimism and enthusiasm.

Artist for Pratipada: Jigardan Gadhavi

Jigardan Gadhavi is a talented Indian playback singer and musician, known for his work in the Gujarati music industry. He has gained recognition for his melodious singing and has contributed to the growing popularity of Gujarati music. Jigardan Gadhavi is celebrated for his versatile vocal abilities and the fusion of contemporary music elements with traditional Gujarati music. He is known for promoting and modernizing Gujarati music, making it more appealing to a broader audience, including the younger generation. He has sung a variety of Gujarati songs, from romantic ballads to lively folk-inspired tracks.
