The eighth day of Navratri: Ashtami

Mahagauri is the eighth form of Goddess Durga. She is known for her divine beauty and purity. Mahagauri is often depicted as a serene and graceful goddess, dressed in white. She symbolizes peace, tranquility, and purity. Devotees worship her for blessings of inner and outer beauty, as well as purity of heart and mind.

Dress Code of the Day: Pink

The color associated with Ashtami is typically pink. These colors represent beauty and love. Peacock green, in particular, signifies the peacock, which is associated with the divine, while pink is often linked to love and compassion. It is an opportunity for devotees to seek her blessings for inner and outer purity, serenity, and a virtuous life. The chosen colors of the day symbolize beauty, love, and compassion associated with this form of the Goddess.

Artist for Pratipada: Aishwarya Majmudar

Aishwarya Majmudar is a talented and accomplished Indian playback singer known for her work in the Gujarati and Hindi music industries. She gained prominence at a young age through her participation in reality singing competitions and has since established herself as a versatile and acclaimed singer. She is known for her versatile singing abilities and has performed in various musical genres, including Gujarati, Hindi, and other regional languages. Her melodious voice and ability to adapt to different musical styles have earned her a significant following.
