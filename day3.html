The third day of Navratri: Tritiya

Chandraghanta is the third form of Goddess Durga. She is depicted with a half-moon (Chandra) shaped like a bell (Ghanta) on her forehead, which gives her the name Chandraghanta. This form of the Goddess is known for her bravery and courage and is believed to bestow strength and protection upon her devotees. She is often shown riding a tiger.

Dress Code of the Day: White

Tritiya is a day of devotion to Goddess Chandraghanta. It is an opportunity for devotees to seek her blessings for strength, courage, and protection and to find peace and purity in their lives. The white color of the day symbolizes these qualities and the divine grace of the Goddess.

Artist for Pratipada: Parthiv Gohil

Parthiv Gohil is an Indian playback singer, musician, and performer, primarily known for his work in the field of music, especially in the Gujarati music industry. He has made a name for himself with his versatile singing, encompassing various genres, and has a significant following, particularly in Gujarat. Parthiv Gohil's singing career has included performances in a wide range of musical styles, including playback singing for Gujarati films, devotional songs, folk music, and fusion music. He has lent his voice to numerous popular Gujarati songs and has gained recognition for his melodious and soulful singing.
