The seventh day of Navratri: Saptami

Kalaratri is the seventh form of Goddess Durga. She is known for her fierce and dark complexion, symbolizing her warrior-like nature. She is often depicted with four arms, holding a sword, a cleaver, a lotus, and a blessing gesture. Kalaratri is the destroyer of ignorance and darkness, and she is believed to protect her devotees from harm and evil forces.

Dress Code of the Day: Green

The color associated with Saptami is green. Green symbolizes growth, fertility, and harmony. It signifies the life-giving and nurturing qualities of nature and reflects the protective aspect of Goddess Kalaratri. Saptami is a day of devotion to Goddess Kalaratri, and it is an opportunity for devotees to seek her blessings for protection, courage, and the power to overcome adversity. The green color of the day symbolizes the nurturing and life-giving qualities of the Goddess.

Artist for Pratipada: Kirtidan Gadhvi

Kirtidan Gadhvi is a well-known and highly respected Indian folk singer and musician, primarily associated with the Gujarati music industry. He is celebrated for his soulful and melodious singing, particularly in the genre of Garba and folk music, which are an integral part of the cultural and musical heritage of Gujarat. While Kirtidan Gadhvi is renowned for his Garba and folk music performances, he is a versatile artist who has also explored other genres of music, including devotional and classical music.
