The fifth day of Navratri: Panchami

Skandamata is the fifth form of Goddess Durga. She is often depicted as a motherly figure holding her son, Lord Skanda or Kartikeya, in her lap. She rides a lion and has four arms, with two of them holding lotus flowers. Skandamata is the motherly and nurturing aspect of the Goddess and represents motherhood, fertility, and protection.

Dress Code of the Day: Blue

Panchami is a day of devotion to Goddess Skandamata, and it is an opportunity for devotees to seek her blessings for the welfare of their family, especially the safety and protection of their children. The blue color of the day symbolizes the vastness and nurturing nature of the Goddess.

Artist for Pratipada: Falguni Pathak

Falguni Pathak is a highly popular and acclaimed Indian singer and musician, particularly known for her contributions to the music and entertainment industry, especially during the festive season of Navratri. She is often referred to as the "Dandiya Queen" for her significant role in popularizing the traditional Gujarati dance form of Garba and Dandiya Raas through her music and live performances.She is renowned for her Garba and Dandiya music, which is celebrated during the Navratri festival in Gujarat and among the Gujarati diaspora. Her live performances during Navratri have garnered her a massive following and turned her into a cultural icon.
