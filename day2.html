The second day of Navratri: Dwitiya

Dwitiya is a day of reverence and devotion to Goddess Brahmacharini. It is an occasion to seek her blessings for spiritual enlightenment, discipline, and the strength to overcome challenges on the path of righteousness.

Dress Code of the Day: ORANGE

Orange is the color associated with the second day of Navratri, representing Goddess Brahmacharini. Orange signifies purity, spiritual growth, and renunciation. It reflects the ascetic and disciplined nature of Goddess Brahmacharini, who is believed to be devoted to penance and austerity.

Artist for Pratipada: Kinjal Dave

Kinjal Dave is a popular Indian singer known for her work in the Gujarati music industry, particularly in the field of Garba and folk music. She gained widespread recognition and popularity for her performances and music, especially during the festive season of Navratri, when Garba and Dandiya Raas dance forms are celebrated with great enthusiasm in Gujarat and among the Gujarati diaspora worldwide. Kinjal Dave's music is often characterized by its energetic beats, traditional folk influences, and contemporary adaptations, making it appealing to a wide range of audiences. Some of her well-known songs and albums include "Char Bangdi Vali Audi," "Leri Lala," and "Rona Ser Ma."
