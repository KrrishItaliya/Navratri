The First day of Navratri: Pratipada

Pratipada is dedicated to the worship of the Goddess Shailputri. This day is marked by various rituals and celebrations, and it holds special significance in the Navratri festival.

Dress Code of the Day: GREY

Grey represents the first day of Navratri and is associated with Goddess Shailputri. Grey symbolizes the quality of stability and harmony. It is believed to represent the tranquil and unchanging nature of the mountains, as Shailputri is often depicted as the daughter of the Himalayas. It signifies the beginning of the festival and the foundation of strength and determination.

Artist for Pratipada: Aditya Gadhvi

Aditya Gadhvi is a playback singer and lyricist from Gujarat, India. He was born on 3 April 1994 in Dudhrej, Gujarat. He started his singing career at a young age and has since become one of the most popular singers in Gujarat. Gadhvi has sung in a variety of genres, including folk, Sufi, and pop. He has also sung in several Indian languages, including Gujarati, Hindi, Marathi, and English. His songs have been featured in films, television shows, and commercials. Some of Gadhvi's most popular songs include "Naagar Nandji Na Laal", "Rang Morla", "Aavi Rudi", and "Gajro". He has also released several successful albums, including "Ochhav" and "Khalasi".
